Every time Flowent shipped an app update I needed a fresh set of store screenshots: the right sizes for the App Store and Google Play, on brand, with readable headlines. Doing that by hand in a design tool is slow, easy to get wrong, and has to be redone for every release. So I built flowent-shotkit to make it a pipeline instead of a design sprint.

## The idea

A store screenshot is not really one image. It is a stack of known layers: a background, a device frame, the app screen inside it, a status bar, and a headline on top. Once each layer is produced in code, making dozens of polished, on-brand slides is a render job rather than a manual task. flowent-shotkit is one TypeScript engine that composes those layers and outputs every store size from a single config.

## What it does

You give it your raw screenshots and a small config, and it renders a full kit: the correct resolutions for each target (for example 1290 by 2796 for the 6.9 inch iPhone, 2064 by 2752 for the 13 inch iPad, and 1080 by 1920 for a Play phone), organised into folders by store, with a contact sheet. It runs with no backend, either from the command line for batch and CI use, or in the browser.

## The studio

Most of the time I use the studio, a local web app. You upload screenshots, edit the headline on each slide or have them written automatically, pick a theme, choose the target stores, and see a live preview. The themes carry the Flowent brand (Montserrat, the blue gradient, a soft glow), with presets to switch the whole look at once. You can toggle the device frame, run A/B copy variants, localise the text, preview every store size, and export the finished kit as a ZIP. The same engine runs in the browser and on the command line, so the studio and CI produce identical output.

## The AI part, kept optional

Writing a good one-line headline for each slide is the slow part, so there is an optional AI director that drafts headlines from the screenshots. It is off by default and uses your own key. Without it the tool still writes sensible headlines offline, so nothing depends on a paid service to work.

## Why I am happy with it

It turned a recurring chore into a few minutes of work and stays on brand by construction. It also reuses cleanly beyond Flowent: the engine is separate from the brand config, so pointing it at another product is mostly a new theme.
