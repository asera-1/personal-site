Most people who study a language can read and write it far better than they can speak it. The reason is simple. Speaking practice is the hardest kind to get. It needs a patient partner, real time, and a setting where it is safe to be wrong, and almost no course gives a learner enough of it. Flowent is the company I started to close that gap.

## What it is

Flowent is an AI speaking practice platform for university and institutional language programs. A learner holds a real spoken conversation with the app, gets corrected, and comes back to practice the things they got wrong. It runs as native iOS and Android apps, with a spaced repetition engine underneath so that practice compounds instead of fading.

## The idea behind it

The product is built on second language acquisition research rather than on a generic chatbot. The parts of that research that shape Flowent are the ones with the strongest evidence. Learners need to produce language, not just consume it. Noticing the gap between what they meant and what they actually managed to say is where a lot of the learning happens. A little difficulty in the right place helps rather than hurts. And spaced repetition is how things move into long term memory. Large language models and voice AI make it possible to deliver all of that at the scale of an app, which was not practical a few years ago.

## Who it is for

Flowent sells to universities and language centres rather than directly to individual learners. These institutions already run language programs, already have the students, and care about outcomes they can measure. We signed our first institutional contract with the Universität Osnabrück Language Centre, which is both a real customer and a place to learn what these buyers actually need.

## My role

I am the founder, and at this stage that means working across the whole company. I set the product direction and build, I talk to language centres and handle the sales, I work on funding, and I shape how Flowent is positioned and explained. The technical background helps here, because the product decisions and the engineering decisions are often the same decision.

## Where it is going

The near term work is turning the first contract into a repeatable way of selling to institutions, and turning early use into evidence that the practice genuinely improves how people speak. The longer term aim is for Flowent to be the speaking part of a language program that an institution can rely on.
