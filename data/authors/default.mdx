I am a co-founder of Flowent, an AI speaking practice platform for university and institutional language programs. My work spans the whole company: product and engineering, conversations with language centres, fundraising, and go to market.

Flowent started from a simple gap. Most language learners read and write far better than they speak, because real speaking practice is hard to come by. Flowent uses large language models and voice AI to give learners structured speaking practice, built on research into how people acquire a second language. We signed our first institutional contract with the Universität Osnabrück Language Centre, and we ship native iOS and Android apps with a spaced repetition engine.

I am studying for an MSc in Cognitive Science, focused on machine learning and AI, at Universität Osnabrück, and I hold a B.Eng. in Computer and Systems Engineering from Al-Azhar University in Cairo. Before Flowent I worked as a Data Steward at Veeva Systems, and I have built projects ranging from time series demand forecasting to a small language model trained from scratch.

I write here about building an AI product as a co-founder, and what I learn along the way.
