Abdalla Sera is the founder of **Flowent**, an AI-powered speaking-practice platform for university and institutional language programs. He is an MSc Cognitive Science student at Universität Osnabrück, focused on machine learning and AI, and holds a B.Eng. in Computer and Systems Engineering from Al-Azhar University in Cairo.

His work sits at the intersection of language, learning science, and applied AI: building products that use large language models and voice AI to give learners real speaking practice, grounded in second-language-acquisition research (output, noticing, desirable difficulties, spaced repetition).

Before Flowent, he worked as a Data Steward at Veeva Systems and built projects ranging from time-series demand forecasting to a GPT-style language model trained from scratch and enterprise network emulations. He writes here about building AI products from a research context.
